AIMS

The African Institute for Mathematical Sciences

Africa has the youngest population in the world. And it is growing. This represents an enormous potential… if only local talents can be given the means to thrive.

What is AIMS?

The African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) is a pan-African network of excellence equipping gifted students to excel, steering the continent towards scientific, educational and economic self-sufficiency. It was created in 2003 by Prof. Neil Turok. Here is what he said during his TED Prize Wish in 2008:

“So I had an idea. And the idea is very simple.
Let’s set up an African Institute for Mathematical Sciences, or AIMS.
Let’s recruit students from the whole of Africa and bring them together with lecturers from all over the world,
and we’ll try to give them a fantastic education.”

Neil Turok

There are now AIMS centres in South Africa, Senegal, Ghana, Cameroon and Rwanda.

AIMS is such a boosting and gratifying environment to teach and learn. It is common to see students and lecturers engaging in passionate conversations during dinners or until late at night in the computer lab.

Whether you are finishing your Ph.D. or soon a retiring professor, there are many options, from giving a remote guest lecture to be stationed in one of the centres as lecturer or tutor! Find more details in Links.
